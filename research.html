Research

PhD Thesis · University of Potsdam · 2022

The Impact of the Socio-Cultural Context on Design Thinking Education

As an educator and practitioner working across very different cultural contexts, I learned early that the experiences I design have to resonate with the people in the room, and that this means adapting to the context I am in. I had felt the other side of it as a student too, navigating my share of cultural mismatches while studying in Tehran, Berlin, Havana, and Istanbul.

My doctoral research took a critical look at how design thinking, a methodology that grew out of a specific Silicon Valley context, travels around the world, and at whether and how local educators adapt its methods, tools, and instructional strategies to their own settings. I spent time inside two design schools, the d-school at the University of Cape Town in South Africa and Genovasi in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, running qualitative interviews and observing classes to see how design thinking is actually taught on the ground.

From that fieldwork I built a model to help educators design learning experiences that resonate with the contexts they serve. Supervised by Prof. Dr. Christoph Meinel and Prof. Dr. Katharina Hölzle, the dissertation is published open access. You can read it online or open the full PDF.

Other Areas of Research

I was a scholarship holder of the HPI-Stanford Design Thinking Research Program, an interdisciplinary collaboration between the Hasso Plattner Institute and Stanford University. There my work centered on online learning and MOOC design, adult learning, experiential learning, the transfer of design thinking skills, and culturally inclusive education.
